Seeing the fantastic variety of materials in our world, its easy to forget that almost all of it arises from interactions of just a few particles: electrons, protons, and neutrons. We know very precisely how these components interact in small populations, but the way macroscopic populations lead to all the diverse behaviors we see is still a mystery.

For most materials, the quantum mechanical nature of the atoms that make up a material is hidden. In some materials, like superconductors, quantum mechanical effects are seen on a macroscopic scale, and lead to new fantastical and useful properties. I refer to these as quantum materials.

This is a simple web site for keeping track of things I've learned by studying quantum materials. My favorite tools are computational in nature, often stochastic, and focus on using data to guide new understanding.
