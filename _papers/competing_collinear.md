In this work, we benchmark diffusion Monte Carlo on the iron-based superconductor, FeSe, and use it to explore the relationship between magnetism, structure, and electronic properties.

### Benchmark

We compared our [QWalk][1] results to [TurboRVB][2] and found that all our overlapping results matched within statistical errors.
We also matched experimental results for the equation of state and found that the DMC esimated bandwith is consistent with experiments.

### Relationship between structure, magnetism, and electronic properties.

Our main results on this relationship are 
* Pressure makes various striped states become very close in energy, while other states increase in energy.
* Local spin order has important affects on the electronic state of the iron atoms.

We investigate this by fixing the local magnetic order of iron square-lattice, and computing energies and other electronic properties as we change the structure.
The results suggest that the magnetic, phonon, and electronic degrees of freedom in FeSe are all coupled, which may explain why this is such an unusual material!
